Portfolio 3: Graphics Town

This portfolio is the Graphics Town “project” from the regular 559 class. It’s become a tradition in class with a long history. Graphics town is an opportunity for students to show off the stuff they’ve learned in class.

For this assignment, there is only one big box: Graphics Town. It can be found on a separate page ( gr-01-01.html) - as well as on Page  1  (Graphics Town).

Rather than a lot of small boxes, this assignment has a single, big window. In said window, you should create a 3D world of your choosing with as much creativity as you want.

We realize that you have limited time to do this so we’ve tried to make our expectations as reasonable as possible.

In the regular class, the assignment gets quite complex with grading requirements. For the honors section, things are simplified. “A"s are obvious (you can look at them and tell that they are “good enough”) and the rest are “not-yet-As”. If your assignment isn’t obviously an A in the regular class, it’s not an A for the honors section.

That being said, we’ve included the regular section’s grading details so that you can get ideas from them and treat them like guidelines for what an “A” grade looks like.

We also recommend that you look at some examples from recent years. The 2023 Galleries are a good start since you can actually try them out. Other years have similar assignments (but usually just pictures): 2024, 2022, 2021, 2019.

We do recommend that you read through all pages of this “workbook” - it has useful ways to think about the assignment. Even though the grading details don’t apply, they can give you ideas.

We encourage honors students to share things (use objects/behaviors from other students). Your world must be your own, but it can have things from others (just make sure to cite where you got the objects/behaviors from).

Requirements and Evaluation

The regular assignment (over the next few pages) gives lots of details. But the things to focus on are:

  1. The UI still works and things run at a reasonable speed.
  2. You have a complex enough world with enough objects.
  3. The world looks intentional - there is some theme or concept behind it (it’s not just a random assortment of stuff).
  4. You have interesting behaviors and motions in the world.
  5. You use the UI elements (highlighting, lookat, riding) to show off the cool objects.
  6. There are some things that are “technically advanced” (see Page  5  (Graphics Town Advanced Things))
  7. There is a shader that you wrote

It should be obvious to the viewer that all these criteria are met.
